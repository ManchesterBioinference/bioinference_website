I am a PhD student in Genomics at the University of Manchester, funded by the International Partnership Scholarship with the University of Heidelberg. I am co-supervised by Nicoletta Bobola and Magnus Rattray. My research focuses on developing statistical methods to identify regulatory gene networks that define tissue identity during human embryonic development. I work with single-cell and spatial transcriptomics data, using Python-based pipelines and probabilistic modelling to study developmental processes and gene regulation in human tissues.

Prior to my PhD, I worked as a Research Assistant at Guangzhou Laboratory, focusing on single-cell multi-omics analysis, including transcriptomics, genomics, and epigenomics using both second- and third-generation sequencing technologies. My research spanned both cancer biology and neurodevelopment.

In cancer studies, I investigated the regulatory impact of extrachromosomal DNA (ecDNA) and structural variants on gene expression at single-cell resolution. I developed computational methods to identify novel RNA structures arising from structural variations and revealed that ecDNA-driven transcriptional amplification is not solely explained by copy number, suggesting more complex regulatory mechanisms. This work was published in Clinical and Translational Medicine, where I am a co-first author.

In parallel, I explored isoform regulation during brain development using single-cell long-read sequencing. I built end-to-end computational pipelines to analyse transcript diversity and identified dynamic isoform switching during neurogenesis. Notably, I observed a global reduction in isoform diversity in autism, potentially linked to epigenetic regulation, highlighting isoform-level dysregulation as a key feature of neurodevelopmental disorders. This work is published in Genomics, Proteomics and Bioinformatics.

These projects provided me with extensive experience in large-scale data analysis, algorithm development, and integrating multi-modal biological data to uncover regulatory mechanisms in complex systems.

In the long term, I aim to uncover fundamental principles of gene regulation in human development through integrative and data-driven approaches. I am particularly interested in linking these mechanisms to developmental diseases at the molecular level. Ultimately, I hope to contribute to AI-driven and computational strategies that support regenerative medicine and more precise, mechanism-based therapies.
